## How to Sign

The Project uses a lightweight, file-based signing process (there is no click-through form):

1. **Read** this document in full.
2. **Add yourself** to the signers list in [`.cla-signers`](.cla-signers): one line with your
   Git hosting username (the same account you open pull requests with). You may do this in the
   same pull request as your first Contribution, or in a separate one.
3. In your first pull request, include the following statement so the intent is recorded in
   the history:

   > I have read the CLA document and I hereby sign the CLA.

By adding your username to `.cla-signers` and/or submitting a Contribution, **You accept and
agree to the terms of this Contributor License Agreement for Your present and future
Contributions to the Project.**

A pull-request check (see `.gitea/workflows/cla.yml`) reports whether the pull-request author
is listed in `.cla-signers`. During the initial rollout this check is **informational and
does not block merges**; the Maintainers may make it a required check later.

### Contributions on behalf of a company (Corporate CLA)

If You are contributing as part of Your employment or on behalf of a legal entity, an
authorized representative of that entity should state so in the pull request and list the
entity name alongside the signing username. Contact the Maintainers via a repository issue to
arrange a Corporate CLA covering all contributors from Your organization.
